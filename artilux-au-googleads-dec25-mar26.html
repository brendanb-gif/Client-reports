CHANGES MADE

Changes made

Actions taken during the period to improve account performance and reduce wasted spend.

✓
Automated search term filtering

An automated system runs daily across both non-branded campaigns, identifying and blocking searches that fall outside the target intent. This progressively reduces wasted clicks over time without requiring manual review of every search.

✕
Manual exclusions added to Door & Window Screens

On top of the automated system, a manual review layer was applied to catch searches that appeared relevant but were leading to low-quality traffic. Additional exclusions were added specifically to the Door & Window Screens campaign.

⟳
Daily budget pacing

Automated daily budget adjustments were applied across both campaigns throughout the period to keep spend on track against monthly targets, preventing over-delivery in high-traffic periods and under-delivery in quieter ones.

WHAT'S NEXT

What's next

Upcoming priorities to bring cost per lead down and grow lead volume heading into the stronger season.

CPL efficiency recovery

Cost per lead has risen from $45 in the prior period to $58. The account's best months have delivered leads at $43–$45. Reviewing the efficiency targets on both non-branded campaigns and refining how they respond to search demand should bring CPL back toward those levels.

Spring season preparation

October is consistently the account's strongest month — 194 leads at $43 per lead last year. Getting campaign structures, budgets, and efficiency settings in place before demand picks up ensures the account is ready to capture that volume rather than adjusting after the fact.

Geographic targeting test

Location-focused campaigns are currently paused. Reintroducing a targeted geographic approach for the highest-converting metro markets could drive incremental lead volume, particularly in areas where search demand is concentrated.

Branded budget review

Branded leads are converting at $20 per lead — roughly a third the cost of non-branded. Reviewing whether the branded campaign has sufficient budget to capture full demand in its category is a straightforward efficiency opportunity that requires no creative changes.
